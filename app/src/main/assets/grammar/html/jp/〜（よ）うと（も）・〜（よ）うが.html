〜（よ）うと（も）・〜（よ）うが

Explanation in Original Language

〜でも、それに関係ない・影響されない。

Meaning and Nuances

This grammar pattern expresses that regardless of whether the action or state described by the verb (in volitional form 〜（よ）う) happens or is true, the result or situation mentioned in the main clause remains unchanged. It emphasizes the irrelevance or lack of influence of the condition stated in the first clause. The nuance is similar to "no matter if/whether X happens..." or "even if X happens...". It often conveys a sense of determination, resignation, stating a general truth, or indifference towards the hypothetical condition.

The `（も）` in `〜（よ）うと（も）` is optional and adds a slight emphasis, akin to "even if." `〜（よ）うが` is very similar in meaning, sometimes considered slightly more direct or colloquial than `〜（よ）うと`. Both are generally used for hypothetical situations that the speaker considers irrelevant to the main point.

Usage in Context

This structure is used in both spoken and written Japanese, though it can sound somewhat formal or emphatic depending on the context. It connects two clauses: the first clause ends with the volitional form of a verb + `と（も）` or `が`, presenting the condition. The second clause states the result or fact that holds true regardless of that condition. It's often used to express strong will, assert a fixed outcome, or dismiss a potential obstacle or concern.

Structure: Verb (volitional form) + と（も）/ が, [Main Clause]

Examples

どんなに反対されようと、私は自分の計画を実行するつもりだ。

No matter how much others may oppose it, I intend to carry out my plan.

Someone expressing strong determination to proceed with their plan despite anticipated opposition.

たとえ雨が降ろうが、明日のハイキングは決行します。

Even if it rains / Whether it rains or not, tomorrow's hike will be held as planned.

Announcing a decision that will not be changed by the weather condition.

彼が何と言おうと（も）、私の気持ちは変わらない。

Whatever he may say, my feelings won't change.

Expressing the firmness of one's own feelings, irrespective of what another person says.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜ても・〜でも: This is the most common way to express "even if." While similar, `〜（よ）うと/が` often carries a stronger sense of "no matter what" or emphasizes the hypothetical condition more forcefully. It can sound more resolute or formal than `〜ても/でも`.

たとえ〜ても: Also means "even if," often emphasizing the hypothetical nature ("even supposing..."). `〜（よ）うと/が` focuses strongly on the irrelevance of the condition to the outcome, sometimes feeling more direct.

〜にしても・〜にしろ: These also mean "even if" or "whether," but are often used when considering alternatives or possibilities. `〜（よ）うと/が` typically focuses on a single stated condition and its lack of effect.

Cultural and Practical Notes

The use of the volitional form (`〜（よ）う`) signifies a hypothetical situation, intent, or potential occurrence. Adding `と（も）` or `が` emphasizes that the main clause holds true *despite* this hypothetical condition. This structure can sound quite determined or even defiant, so it's important to use it in appropriate contexts. It's often used when the speaker anticipates the condition mentioned but wants to stress it won't affect their actions or the outcome.
