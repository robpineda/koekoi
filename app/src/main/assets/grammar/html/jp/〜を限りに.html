〜を限りに

Explanation in Original Language

〜の時までで、それまで続いていたことを終わりにする、と宣言する。

Meaning and Nuances

This declares that something ends at a specific point (〜), marking a definitive conclusion to an ongoing action or state. It often implies a deliberate decision or formal announcement.

Usage in Context

Used in announcements, personal resolutions, or formal statements about endings, often with a sense of finality.

Examples

今月を限りに、この店を閉店します。

We will close this shop as of this month.

Business closure notice.

今日を限りに、タバコをやめると決めた。

I’ve decided to quit smoking as of today.

Personal resolution.

この試合を限りに、彼は引退を表明した。

He announced his retirement with this match as the end.

Sports farewell.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜をもって: Similar “ending” meaning, but more formal and often used for events or official matters.

〜で終わり: More casual, lacking the declarative tone.

Cultural and Practical Notes

The declarative nature aligns with Japan’s respect for clear boundaries in commitments. Useful for N1 writing or formal listening.
