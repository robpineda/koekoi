〜てからというもの（は）

Explanation in Original Language

〜してからある変化が起こり、その後ずっと同じ状態が続いている。

Meaning and Nuances

"〜てからというもの（は）" (te kara to iu mono (wa)) means "ever since X happened..." or "after X happened, ... (and continuing in that state)". It emphasizes that a specific event or action (indicated by the verb in て-form) marked a turning point, initiating a new state or situation that has continued unchanged up to the present. It highlights both the **triggering event** and the **subsequent continuous state**. The optional は (wa) adds emphasis. It often implies a significant change in lifestyle, habit, or condition.

Usage in Context

This grammar pattern is used to describe a distinct change that occurred after a specific point in the past, with the resulting state persisting over time. It's common in both spoken and written Japanese when reflecting on changes. It's often used to talk about personal experiences, changes in health, habits, or circumstances. The clause following 〜てからというもの（は） describes the ongoing state.

Structure: Verb (て-Form) + からというもの（は）+ Clause Describing Continuous State

Examples

日本に来てからというもの、毎日日本のニュースを見るようになった。

Ever since I came to Japan, I've started watching Japanese news every day.

Describing a new habit that started upon arrival in Japan and has continued since.

子供が生まれてからというもの、生活がすっかり変わってしまった。

Since the child was born, my life has completely changed (and remains changed).

Reflecting on the significant and ongoing life changes after having a baby.

あのレストランで食事してからというもの、すっかりそこのファンになった。

Ever since eating at that restaurant, I've become a complete fan of the place.

Explaining how a single experience led to a continuous state of being a fan.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜てから (te kara): Simply means "after doing X." It indicates sequence but doesn't inherently emphasize the continuation of the resulting state like 〜てからというもの does. Example: 宿題をしてから寝た (I went to sleep after doing homework) - just a sequence.
〜て以来 (te irai): Very similar to 〜てからというもの, meaning "since doing X." Both indicate a starting point and a continuing state. 〜て以来 might be perceived as slightly more formal or literary than 〜てからというもの. Both are largely interchangeable in many contexts emphasizing continuation.
〜のをきっかけに (no o kikkake ni): Means "with X as a trigger/opportunity." It focuses more on the event that initiated the change, while 〜てからというもの focuses more on the continuous state *after* the event.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This pattern is useful for emphasizing a lasting change resulting from a specific past event. It adds more weight than a simple "〜てから." It often carries a slightly nostalgic or reflective tone when looking back at how things have changed and stayed that way since a particular point in time. The state described after 〜てからというもの is usually something significant and noticeable.
