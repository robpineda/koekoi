〜きらいがある

Explanation in Original Language

〜という良くない傾向・性質・くせがある。

Meaning and Nuances

The grammar point 「〜きらいがある」 (kirai ga aru) is used to indicate that someone or something has an undesirable tendency, nature, or habit. It points out a negative characteristic or inclination that is often recurring or inherent. The nuance is generally one of mild criticism, disapproval, or concern about this tendency. It's often used for characteristics that are considered flaws or weaknesses.

While the word 「嫌い」(kirai) usually means "dislike" or "hate," in this grammatical structure, it signifies a "tendency" or "inclination," specifically a negative one. It's somewhat formal and often found in written language or more formal speech when analyzing behavior or characteristics.

Usage in Context

「〜きらいがある」 is typically used in contexts where one is observing or commenting on a recurring negative pattern or characteristic. This could be in essays, reports, character analysis, or formal discussions about problems or weaknesses. It's less common in very casual, everyday conversation among close friends, where simpler expressions might be preferred.

It attaches to:

  • The dictionary form of a verb (e.g., 彼は考えすぎるきらいがある - He has a tendency to overthink).
  • A noun + の particle (e.g., あの政策は現実無視のきらいがある - That policy has a tendency to ignore reality).

It's often used to talk about the habits or tendencies of people (including oneself, expressing self-awareness of a flaw), groups, systems, or abstract concepts.

Examples

彼は何事も考えすぎるきらいがある。

He has a tendency to overthink everything.

Describing a friend or colleague who often gets bogged down in details or worries excessively before acting.

最近の若者は、本をあまり読まないきらいがあると言われている。

It is said that young people recently have a tendency not to read books very much.

Commenting on a perceived general trend or habit among a specific demographic group, often heard in social commentary.

この種の報告書は、詳細を省略するきらいがあるので注意が必要だ。

You need to be careful as these kinds of reports have a tendency to omit details.

Warning someone about a potential flaw or negative characteristic inherent in a particular type of document or system.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜がち (gachi): Also indicates a tendency, often negative, but is generally less formal and more common for frequently occurring, less serious habits or states (e.g., 病気がち - prone to illness, 忘れがち - tend to forget). 「きらいがある」 often implies a more significant or ingrained negative trait or characteristic and sounds more formal/written.

〜傾向がある (keikou ga aru): Means "there is a tendency/trend." This expression is more neutral and objective than 「きらいがある」. It simply states that a tendency exists, without the inherent negative judgment carried by 「きらいがある」. It can be used for positive, negative, or neutral tendencies.

〜っぽい (ppoi): Means "-ish," "like," or "prone to." While it can sometimes imply a negative tendency (e.g., 怒りっぽい - quick to anger, 忘れっぽい - forgetful), it's much more colloquial and broader in meaning than 「きらいがある」. It doesn't inherently carry the same level of criticism or formality.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Remember that 「〜きらいがある」 carries a distinctly negative connotation. Do not use it to describe neutral or positive tendencies; use 「傾向がある」 instead for neutral observations. Due to its critical nuance and somewhat formal tone, be mindful when using it to describe others directly, especially superiors, as it can sound judgmental. It's often safer to use it for general observations, third parties not present, or even for self-criticism.
