〜をものともせず（に）

Explanation in Original Language

普通なら〜という障害に気持ちが負けてしまうのに、それを乗り越えて行動する。

Meaning and Nuances

This means acting “without being deterred by 〜,” overcoming obstacles that would typically hinder others. It conveys courage or resilience.

Usage in Context

Used in narratives or praise to highlight bravery or determination, often dramatically.

Examples

危険をものともせず、彼は火の中に飛び込んだ。

Undeterred by danger, he leapt into the fire.

Heroic act.

批判をものともせずに、彼女は意見を述べ続けた。

Unfazed by criticism, she kept voicing her opinions.

Resolve.

疲労をものともせず、走り続けた。

He kept running, undeterred by exhaustion.

Endurance.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜にもかかわらず: “Despite,” but less focused on overcoming.

〜を恐れず: “Without fear,” narrower in scope.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Celebrates perseverance, a valued trait in Japan. Useful for N1 dramatic reading.
