〜なり…なり

Explanation in Original Language

〜でもいいし…でもいいから、何かをする。

Meaning and Nuances

The grammar structure 「Aなり Bなり」 is used to list two or more examples (A and B) of possible actions or choices, implying that doing *at least one* of them (or something similar) is suggested or required. It conveys the sense of "either A or B, or something along those lines." It often carries a nuance of urging someone to take *some* action from the presented options, without strictly limiting them to only those options. It suggests that the specific choice isn't as important as the act of doing *something* from that category. It's generally neutral to slightly informal in tone.

Usage in Context

「〜なり…なり」 is typically used when giving advice, making suggestions, or indicating possible courses of action, especially when the speaker wants *some* kind of response or action without dictating the exact one. It often follows the dictionary form of verbs or nouns. The structure is typically: `Verb (dict. form) + なり + Verb (dict. form) + なり + (する/してください/しろ etc.)` or `Noun + なり + Noun + なり`. It's more common in spoken Japanese or informal writing than in highly formal contexts.

Examples

熱があるなら、薬を飲むなり、寝るなりしたほうがいいですよ。

If you have a fever, you should probably take some medicine or get some sleep (or do something similar to recover).

Giving advice to someone who is feeling unwell, suggesting possible actions for recovery.

分からないことがあれば、先生に聞くなり、自分で調べるなりしてください。

If there's something you don't understand, please either ask the teacher or look it up yourself.

A teacher instructing students on how to handle questions or problems they encounter.

この書類、コピーするなりファイルするなり、早く処理してくれ。

This document - either copy it or file it, just process it quickly.

A boss giving instructions to an employee, emphasizing the need for action rather than the specific method.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜とか〜とか: Lists examples non-exhaustively, often more casually. It doesn't carry the same implication of urging action as 「〜なり…なり」. Example: 「週末は映画を見るとか、本を読むとかして過ごす。」(On weekends, I spend time doing things like watching movies or reading books.)
〜か〜か: Presents a more definitive choice between two specific options (A or B). 「〜なり…なり」 is softer, suggesting examples rather than a strict choice. Example: 「コーヒーか紅茶か、どちらにしますか？」(Coffee or tea, which would you like?)
〜やら〜やら: Lists multiple things, often with a nuance of being overwhelmed, disorganized, or dealing with many things at once. 「〜なり…なり」 focuses on suggesting alternative actions. Example: 「レポートやらプレゼンの準備やらで忙しい。」(I'm busy with things like the report, presentation prep, and so on.)

Cultural and Practical Notes

The key point of 「〜なり…なり」 is the suggestion to take *some* action from a category represented by the examples. It's less about a strict choice and more about prompting activity. Be careful not to use it when a very specific action is required. It implies a degree of freedom for the listener to choose how they proceed, as long as they do *something* appropriate.
