〜なり…なり

Explanation in Original Language

〜でもいいし…でもいいから、何かをする。

Meaning and Nuances

This suggests options (〜 or …) where either is acceptable, often urging action regardless of choice. It’s casual and flexible, implying “whichever works.”

Usage in Context

Used in informal speech to propose alternatives or suggest indifference to specifics.

Examples

紅茶なりコーヒーなり、何か飲みたい。

I want something to drink, tea or coffee, whatever.

Casual request.

本なり雑誌なり、読むものを持ってきて。

Bring something to read, a book or a magazine, anything.

Laid-back instruction.

犬なり猫なり、ペットを飼いたい。

I want to keep a pet, a dog or a cat, either one.

Pet preference.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜か…か: Simpler “or,” without the casual urging tone.

〜でも…でも: More emphatic “even if,” less about options.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Reflects casual flexibility in Japanese. Useful for N1 informal dialogue.
