〜にあって

Explanation in Original Language

〜のような特別な状況だからこそあることが起こる。〜のような特別な状況でもあることが起こる。

Meaning and Nuances

This highlights that something occurs precisely because of a specific, often exceptional situation (〜). It implies the event is unique to that context, with a formal or serious tone. It can also suggest “in the midst of” a situation.

Usage in Context

Found in formal writing (e.g., reports, essays) or serious discussions, it’s rare in casual speech.

Examples

戦争にあって、人々は団結して困難を乗り越えた。

In the midst of war, people united to overcome hardships.

Historical reflection on resilience.

災害にあって、彼の優しさが初めて分かった。

It was during the disaster that I first understood his kindness.

A special situation revealing character.

会議にあって、重要な決定が下された。

During the meeting, an important decision was made.

Formal reporting of events.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜において: More general “in/at” a situation, without the causal nuance of 〜にあって.

〜だからこそ: Emphasizes “precisely because,” but is less formal and situational.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Its formality ties to Japan’s respect for context in official discourse. For N1, it’s key in reading formal texts or writing sophisticated responses.
