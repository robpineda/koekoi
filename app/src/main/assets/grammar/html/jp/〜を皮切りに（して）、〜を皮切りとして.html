〜を皮切りに（して）、〜を皮切りとして

Explanation in Original Language

〜から始まって次々に何かをする。

Meaning and Nuances

This structure indicates that something begins with 〜 and is followed by a series of subsequent actions or events. It suggests a starting point that triggers a chain reaction, often with a sense of momentum. Both forms (〜を皮切りに and 〜を皮切りとして) are interchangeable, though 〜を皮切りに is slightly more common and concise.

Usage in Context

Frequently used in formal or semi-formal contexts like news, speeches, or historical accounts to mark the beginning of a sequence.

Examples

新しい工場建設を皮切りに、地域の経済が活性化した。

Starting with the construction of a new factory, the regional economy revitalized.

Economic development narrative.

彼の引退を皮切りとして、チームの若返りが進んだ。

His retirement marked the beginning of the team’s rejuvenation.

Sports or organizational shift.

初公演を皮切りに、全国ツアーがスタートした。

The first performance kicked off a nationwide tour.

Entertainment or event planning.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜をきっかけに: Similar “trigger” meaning, but less formal and doesn’t imply a series of events as strongly.

〜から始まり: More neutral and casual, lacking the momentum nuance.

Cultural and Practical Notes

“皮切り” (literally “skin-cutting”) originates from traditional rituals, reflecting a cultural emphasis on ceremonial beginnings. Useful for N1 reading comprehension in formal texts.
