〜だに

Explanation in Original Language

〜だけでもそのような状況なのだから、実際は極端だ・（「〜だに…ない」の形で）全く…ない。

Meaning and Nuances

「〜だに (dani)」 is a formal and somewhat archaic grammar pattern with two main meanings:

  1. "Even just doing/thinking/hearing ~ causes a strong reaction": It emphasizes that the mere thought, imagination, or mention of something is enough to provoke a significant feeling (like fear, disgust, joy). It implies the actual experience would be far more intense. Used with verbs related to perception or thought (e.g., 思う, 聞く, 想像する).
  2. (In the form 「〜だに…ない」) "Not even ~": Similar to 「〜すら…ない」 or 「〜さえ…ない」, meaning "not even" or "not at all." This usage is less common now but appears in set phrases or older texts.

Overall, it carries a strong sense of formality and is primarily found in written language or very formal/rhetorical speech.

Usage in Context

For the first meaning ("even just thinking/hearing"), 「〜だに」 usually follows the dictionary form or stem of verbs like 「想像する (souzou suru - to imagine)」, 「聞く (kiku - to hear)」, 「考える (kangaeru - to think)」, 「口にする (kuchi ni suru - to mention)」. The end of the sentence often describes an emotional reaction (e.g., 恐ろしい - osoroshii - frightening, 感動する - kandou suru - be moved). The second meaning ("not even") typically appears in negative constructions like 「夢にだに思わなかった (yume ni dani omowanakatta - I didn't even dream of it)」.

Examples

その事故の現場を想像するだに、恐ろしくなる。

Even just imagining the scene of that accident is terrifying.

Emphasizing the horror evoked merely by the thought of the accident scene.

彼の名前を聞くだに、あの日の屈辱がよみがえる。

Even just hearing his name brings back the humiliation of that day.

Highlighting a strong negative reaction triggered simply by hearing a name.

彼がそのような大役を任されるとは、夢にだに思わなかった。

I never even dreamed that he would be entrusted with such an important role.

Using the negative form 「〜だに…ない」 to express complete surprise or disbelief.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜さえ (sae): While 「〜さえ」 can mean "even," it doesn't specifically carry the nuance of "even just the thought/mention of" causing a strong reaction in the same way 「〜だに」 does in its primary usage. 「想像しさえすれば怖い」 is possible but less common and less evocative than 「想像するだに恐ろしい」.

〜すら (sura): Similar to 「〜さえ」, 「〜すら」 means "even" and highlights an extreme example, but usually doesn't focus specifically on the act of thinking/hearing/imagining as the trigger for a reaction like 「〜だに」 does. In the negative form ("not even"), 「〜すら…ない」 and 「〜だに…ない」 can be similar, but 「〜だに…ない」 feels more archaic or literary.

Cultural and Practical Notes

「〜だに」 is a very formal and literary expression (JLPT N1 level). It is rarely, if ever, used in modern casual conversation. Its use lends a heightened, sometimes dramatic or archaic, tone to writing or formal speech. Be cautious when using it; stick to established patterns like 「想像するだに〜」 or 「夢にだに〜ない」 unless you are comfortable with classical Japanese nuances.
