〜をもって

Explanation in Original Language

〜の時までで、ある行事やそれまで続いていたことを終わりにする、と宣言する。

Meaning and Nuances

Similar to 〜を限りに, this marks the end of something at a specific point (〜), but it’s more formal and often tied to official events, ceremonies, or significant conclusions. It has a solemn or authoritative tone.

Usage in Context

Appears in official announcements, legal contexts, or ceremonial closings, less in casual speech.

Examples

本日をもって、すべての業務を終了いたします。

With today, all operations will cease.

Formal business closure.

この手紙をもって、契約を解除します。

With this letter, the contract is terminated.

Legal or official notice.

式典をもって、プロジェクトが完結した。

The project concluded with the ceremony.

Official event wrap-up.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜を限りに: Less formal, more personal or general than 〜をもって.

〜で終わる: Neutral and casual, without the formal declaration.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Its formality reflects Japan’s structured approach to endings in official settings. Essential for N1 formal reading or writing.
