〜といえども

Explanation in Original Language

〜は事実ではあるが・事実であっても・〜の立場の人であっても・実際はそこから普通に予想されることとは違う。

Meaning and Nuances

「〜といえども」 is a formal and somewhat literary concessive conjunction meaning "even if," "even though," "despite being," or "no matter how." Similar to 「〜とはいえ」, it acknowledges a fact, status, or condition, but then introduces a contrasting reality or exception. It often implies that despite the acknowledged premise (which might suggest a certain outcome), the reality is different.

Compared to 「〜とはいえ」, 「〜といえども」 often carries a stronger, more emphatic nuance and is typically used with more extreme conditions, high statuses, or situations where the contrast is particularly notable. It suggests that the condition mentioned is significant, yet *even* that condition doesn't prevent the contrasting reality.

Usage in Context

「〜といえども」 is primarily used in formal writing, speeches, legal documents, and literary texts. It is less common in everyday casual conversation. It typically follows a noun, pronoun, or sometimes a phrase that represents the status or condition being conceded.

Structure: `[Noun/Pronoun/Phrase (representing status/condition)] + といえども、+ [Contrasting Reality/Exception]`

It is often used with words like 「いかに」 (no matter how), 「たとえ」 (even if), or 「何人」 (whoever) to emphasize the concessive nature.

Examples

いかに困難な状況といえども、希望を捨ててはならない。

No matter how difficult the situation may be, one must not abandon hope.

Emphasizing resilience despite extreme hardship.

専門家といえども、常に最新の情報を学び続ける必要がある。

Even experts need to constantly continue learning the latest information.

Stating a requirement that applies even to those with high status/expertise.

たとえ相手が子供といえども、約束は守るべきだ。

Even if the other party is a child, promises should be kept.

Arguing for upholding principles regardless of the other person's status.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜とはいえ: Similar meaning but less formal and generally less emphatic. 「〜とはいえ」 is more versatile for everyday concessions, while 「〜といえども」 tends to be reserved for more formal or stark contrasts.

〜であっても / 〜でも: These also mean "even if" or "even." 「〜といえども」 often sounds more formal, written, or slightly archaic compared to these more common forms, especially 「〜でも」.

〜にしても / 〜にしろ / 〜にせよ: These forms often mean "even if" or "whether," sometimes implying a choice between options or hypothetical situations. 「〜といえども」 typically concedes a single, often factual or status-related point.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Due to its formal nature, 「〜といえども」 lends a sense of gravity or strong conviction to a statement. It is often used when discussing principles, duties, or exceptional circumstances. Overusing it in casual contexts would sound unnatural or overly dramatic.
