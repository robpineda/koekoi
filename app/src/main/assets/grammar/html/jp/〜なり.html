〜なり

Explanation in Original Language

〜という動作にすぐ連続して次のことをする。

Meaning and Nuances

This expresses that one action (〜) is immediately followed by another in a seamless sequence. It suggests spontaneity or a natural flow, often with a sense of urgency or habit. It’s somewhat formal and more common in written or expressive contexts. The verb before 〜なり is in dictionary form.

Usage in Context

Used in narratives or when describing quick, consecutive actions, it’s less common in casual speech where 〜てから might replace it.

Examples

家に着くなり、靴を脱いでソファに倒れ込んだ。

The moment I got home, I took off my shoes and collapsed on the sofa.

Exhaustion driving an immediate sequence of actions.

目が覚めるなり、彼は時計を見て慌てた。

Right upon waking, he looked at the clock and panicked.

A sudden realization prompting quick action.

店に入るなり、店員が笑顔で迎えてくれた。

As soon as I entered the shop, the clerk greeted me with a smile.

A warm, immediate welcome in a service setting.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜が早いか: Similar immediacy, but 〜なり feels more natural and less dramatic.

〜てすぐに: More casual and explicit about the short time gap, lacking 〜なり’s literary tone.

Cultural and Practical Notes

〜なり reflects a Japanese appreciation for smooth transitions in storytelling. It’s useful for JLPT reading but less practical for speaking unless aiming for a refined tone.
