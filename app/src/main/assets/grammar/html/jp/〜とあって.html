〜とあって

Explanation in Original Language

〜という特別な状況だから、当然結果も特別だ。

Meaning and Nuances

`〜とあって` indicates that because of a specific, often special or unusual, situation or circumstance described before it, a certain natural or expected consequence follows. It translates to "due to the special situation that...", "given that...", or "since it's a case of...". The nuance is that the situation mentioned is somewhat out of the ordinary, leading to a result that is also noteworthy or understandable *in light of* that special situation. It implies a logical connection between a unique circumstance and its outcome.

Usage in Context

This pattern is often used to explain why something is happening or why a particular state exists, especially when the reason is a notable event, condition, or time. It is commonly found in explanatory contexts, news reports, descriptions, and narratives, both written and spoken, though it leans slightly towards formality. It typically follows a noun or a plain form verb/adjective clause that describes the special situation.

  • Noun + とあって
  • Verb (plain form) + とあって
  • い-Adjective (plain form) + とあって
  • な-Adjective (plain form, often + な) + とあって

The clause following `〜とあって` describes the natural consequence or resulting state.

Examples

今日は夏休み最後の日曜日とあって、遊園地は朝から大変な混雑だ。

Given that today is the last Sunday of summer vacation, the amusement park has been incredibly crowded since morning.

Explaining the reason for the unusually large crowd at the amusement park – the special circumstance of it being the last Sunday of vacation.

人気アイドルが来日するとあって、空港には多くのファンが詰めかけた。

As a popular idol was visiting Japan, many fans gathered at the airport.

Explaining the large gathering of fans by citing the special event of the idol's arrival.

久しぶりの快晴とあって、公園にはピクニックを楽しむ家族連れが多かった。

Since it was the first clear weather in a while, there were many families enjoying picnics in the park.

Explaining the high number of people in the park due to the special condition of it being a rare sunny day after a period of bad weather.

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • 〜から / 〜ので: These indicate general reasons without the specific nuance of a "special situation" leading to a "natural consequence." `〜とあって` implies the situation itself is somewhat noteworthy.
  • 〜ため（に）: Indicates a reason or cause, often more formally or objectively. While it can state a reason, it doesn't strongly carry the nuance of the situation being particularly special or unusual like `〜とあって` often does.
  • 〜だけに: Also indicates a reason leading to a natural or expected outcome, often emphasizing the degree. "Precisely because A, it's all the more B". `〜とあって` focuses more on the situation itself being special, while `〜だけに` often emphasizes the degree of the outcome.

Cultural and Practical Notes

`〜とあって` is useful for providing context and explaining outcomes based on specific, often publicly known or easily understandable, special circumstances (like holidays, sales, special events, weather conditions). It implies that the resulting situation is a natural and expected consequence *given* those specific circumstances. It often appears in sentences describing a scene or situation resulting from the stated cause.
