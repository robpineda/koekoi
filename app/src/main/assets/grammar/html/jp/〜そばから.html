〜そばから

Explanation in Original Language

〜をしても、すぐにそれに対抗するような動きがあり、それを何度も繰り返す。

Meaning and Nuances

This describes a frustrating cycle where one action (〜) is quickly undone or countered by another, often repeatedly. It conveys futility or irritation, as efforts are negated almost instantly. The verb before 〜そばから is in the te-form.

Usage in Context

Common in daily life descriptions, it’s conversational yet expressive, often with a tone of complaint or exasperation.

Examples

掃除してそばから、子供がまたおもちゃを散らかす。

No sooner do I clean than the kids scatter their toys again.

A parent’s frustration with endless mess.

皿を洗ってそばから、夫が新しい汚れ物を出してくる。

Right after I wash the dishes, my husband brings more dirty ones.

Domestic irritation over undone effort.

説明してそばから、彼は質問を忘れてしまう。

As soon as I explain, he forgets the question.

A teacher or colleague’s exasperation.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜ても: Indicates a simple contrast, but lacks the immediacy and repetition of 〜そばから.

〜た途端に: Focuses on a single immediate reaction, not a repeated cycle.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This reflects a cultural tendency to vocalize life’s small frustrations humorously. It’s practical for speaking and writing at N1 level, especially in relatable scenarios.
