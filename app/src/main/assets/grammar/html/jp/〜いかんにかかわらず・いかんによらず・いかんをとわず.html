〜いかんにかかわらず・いかんによらず・いかんをとわず

Explanation in Original Language

あることの成立に〜は関係ない・影響はない。

Meaning and Nuances

These expressions, 「〜いかんにかかわらず」(ikan ni kakawarazu), 「〜いかんによらず」(ikan ni yorazu), and 「〜いかんをとわず」(ikan o towazu), all mean "regardless of ~," "irrespective of ~," or "no matter what ~ is." They indicate that the factor mentioned before them has no bearing or influence on the main statement or outcome described in the rest of the sentence. They emphasize that something will happen or be true no matter the condition, state, or nature of the preceding noun phrase. These are formal expressions.

While largely interchangeable, 「〜いかんをとわず」 might sometimes place slightly more emphasis on not questioning or considering the details of the preceding factor.

Usage in Context

These grammar points are used primarily in formal writing, official regulations, announcements, legal documents, and formal speech. They are rarely used in casual conversation. They always follow a noun or noun phrase that represents the factor being disregarded. The structure is typically `Noun + いかんにかかわらず / いかんによらず / いかんをとわず, [Main Clause]`. The Kanji form 「〜如何に(拘わらず/依らず/問わず)」 is also common.

Examples

理由のいかんにかかわらず、締め切り後の提出は一切認められません。

Regardless of the reason, submissions after the deadline will not be accepted under any circumstances.

A strict rule stated in guidelines for submitting applications or assignments.

経験のいかんによらず、やる気のある方を募集しています。

We are recruiting motivated individuals, irrespective of their experience.

A line from a job advertisement emphasizing openness to candidates with varying levels of experience.

国籍のいかんをとわず、この施設はどなたでもご利用いただけます。

This facility is available for anyone to use, regardless of nationality.

Information provided at a public facility indicating universal access.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜に関係なく (ni kankei naku): Means "regardless of" or "unrelated to." It's very similar in meaning and can often be used interchangeably. However, 「〜いかんにかかわらず」 and its variants might sound slightly more formal or emphatic.

〜を問わず (o towazu): Often used with categories like age, gender, experience (e.g., 年齢を問わず - regardless of age). It's very similar to 「いかんをとわず」 and generally interchangeable when referring to categories or conditions. 「いかんをとわず」 might be slightly broader, applying also to reasons or circumstances.

どんな〜でも (donna ~ demo): Means "no matter what kind of ~." This is a more general and slightly less formal way to express "regardless," often used in spoken language as well (e.g., どんな理由でも - whatever the reason).

Cultural and Practical Notes

These expressions strongly convey impartiality or universality, stating that a rule, policy, or situation applies across the board without exception based on the specified factor. They are common in contexts where fairness, equality, or strict adherence to rules needs to be emphasized. Because of their formality, learners should primarily expect to encounter them in written materials or formal announcements.
