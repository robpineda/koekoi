〜ともなると・〜ともなれば

Explanation in Original Language

〜くらい立場・程度が高くなると、そのような状態になる。

Meaning and Nuances

〜ともなると and 〜ともなれば indicate that when a certain condition is met, particularly one involving reaching a higher status, level, age, position, or degree of significance, a particular consequence, behavior, or state naturally follows or becomes expected. It implies crossing a threshold into a state where things are different or specific expectations apply.

The nuance is that reaching this level (〜) inherently brings about the subsequent situation. 〜ともなると states this as a general fact or consequence ("When it becomes X..."). 〜ともなれば often carries a slightly more conditional or hypothetical nuance ("If it should become X..." or "Once it gets to the point of being X..."). Both are generally used for significant levels or positions.

Usage in Context

This grammar pattern is often used in more formal writing or speech to describe general truths, societal expectations, or common occurrences associated with reaching a particular stage or status. It typically follows a noun indicating the level, status, time, or position.

The common sentence structure is:

[Noun indicating level/status/time] + ともなると / ともなれば + [Consequence / Expected Situation / Natural Outcome]

Examples

社長ともなると、自分の時間を持つのは難しくなるものです。

When one becomes company president, it naturally becomes difficult to have personal time.

Explaining a common consequence associated with achieving a high corporate position.

子供も高校生ともなれば、自分の将来について真剣に考え始めるだろう。

Once children reach high school age, they will likely start thinking seriously about their future.

Describing an expected developmental milestone associated with reaching a certain age/grade level.

普段は温厚な彼ともなると、家族のことになると人が変わったように怒ることがある。

Even for someone as usually gentle as him, when it comes to matters concerning his family, he sometimes gets angry as if he's a different person.

Highlighting how reaching a certain point (the topic becoming family-related) changes even a calm person's behavior.

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • 〜になると / 〜になれば: Simply means "when/if X becomes Y" or "when it becomes X time". It is more general and neutral. 〜ともなると/〜ともなれば specifically emphasize the significance or high level of the condition (X) and the natural or expected consequences that follow *because* it has reached that level.
  • 〜くらいになると: Similar in meaning ("when it reaches the level of X"), but 〜ともなると/〜ともなれば often sounds more formal and perhaps emphasizes the associated responsibilities or inevitable changes more strongly.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This structure conveys a sense of reaching a significant stage where certain realities or expectations naturally apply. It's often used to talk about roles (manager, parent, adult), age milestones (teenager, elderly), or specific circumstances (exam time, emergencies). 〜ともなれば can sometimes imply a stronger conditionality or a hypothetical future state compared to the more declarative 〜ともなると.
