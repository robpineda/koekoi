〜ところを

Explanation in Original Language

〜の時なのに・〜という事情があるのに、迷惑をかけて恐縮だ。

Meaning and Nuances

The grammar point 「〜ところを」 expresses that an action (usually by the speaker or someone else) happens *despite* the specific situation or moment (the 「ところ」) being inconvenient, inappropriate, or otherwise notable. It often carries a nuance of apology, gratitude for someone's trouble, politeness when interrupting, or sometimes surprise/embarrassment at witnessing something.

It literally means "at the point/moment where...", but implies "even though it was at the point where...". It's frequently used when making requests during a busy time, thanking someone for help given under difficult circumstances, or apologizing for an intrusion.

Usage in Context

「〜ところを」 is commonly used in polite spoken Japanese and formal writing. It connects a clause describing the specific situation (often using Verb-ている, Verb-た, い-Adjective, な-Adjective + な, or Noun + の) to the main action or observation.

Common patterns include:

  • Verb-ているところを〜 (while doing...)
  • Verb-たところを〜 (just after doing...)
  • Adjective-いところを〜 (while it is...)
  • Noun + のところを〜 (at the time of...)

It emphasizes the speaker's awareness of and consideration for the other person's situation.

Examples

お忙しいところを申し訳ありませんが、少しお時間をいただけますでしょうか。

I apologize for bothering you when you are busy, but could I possibly have a little of your time?

Making a polite request to someone who appears occupied.

お休み中のところを、わざわざお越しいただき恐縮です。

I am very grateful that you came all the way here, especially during your time off.

Expressing gratitude to someone who visited despite it being their day off.

危ないところを助けていただいて、本当にありがとうございました。

Thank you so much for saving me when I was in danger.

Thanking someone for help received during a perilous moment.

彼が彼女にプロポーズしているところを、偶然見てしまった。

I accidentally saw him proposing to her.

Expressing mild embarrassment or surprise at witnessing a private moment.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜のに: While both express contrast ("even though"), 「のに」 is more general. 「〜ところを」 specifically highlights the inconvenient or inappropriate *timing* or *situation* of the action that follows and often carries a stronger nuance of apology or politeness due to that specific circumstance.

〜中（に）: Indicates something happens *during* an activity (e.g., 会議中に - during the meeting). While similar, 「〜ところを」 emphasizes interrupting or acting *despite* that specific state, often with an apologetic tone (e.g., 会議中のところを失礼します - Excuse me for interrupting during your meeting).

Cultural and Practical Notes

This structure is fundamental for expressing politeness and consideration in Japanese, especially in formal or business contexts. Using it shows awareness of the other person's circumstances when making requests or expressing gratitude.

Common set phrases include:

  • お忙しいところ (すみません/恐縮ですが...) - (Sorry to bother you) when you're busy...
  • お楽しみのところ (申し訳ありませんが...) - (I apologize for interrupting) while you're enjoying yourselves...
  • お疲れのところ (恐れ入りますが...) - (I'm sorry to ask) when you must be tired...
