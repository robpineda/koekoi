〜たるもの（は）

Explanation in Original Language

〜のような責任のある立場・優れた立場には、そのようなことがふさわしい。

Meaning and Nuances

〜たるもの（は） is a formal expression used to define or emphasize the duties, responsibilities, ideals, or expected standards of behavior appropriate for someone holding a particular position, status, or role, especially one that is respected, authoritative, or carries significant responsibility. It essentially means "One who is X..." or "As someone who holds the position/role of X...", setting a standard or expectation.

The nuance is prescriptive – it states what *should* be the case or what qualities/actions are considered fitting for the role. The （は） particle adds emphasis but is often omitted. It implies a sense of inherent duty or character associated with the position.

Usage in Context

This grammar pattern is primarily used in formal speech and writing, such as in ethical codes, discussions about principles, defining roles, or expressing ideals. It sounds quite stately and perhaps slightly archaic.

The structure is typically:

[Noun indicating position/role/status] + たるもの（は） + [Expected Behavior / Duty / Principle / Characteristic]

It often appears with verbs expressing obligation or necessity like 〜なければならない (must do), 〜べきだ (should do), or states a defining characteristic.

Examples

リーダーたるものは、常に冷静沈着であるべきだ。

Those who are leaders should always be calm and composed.

Stating an ideal characteristic or required behavior for anyone in a leadership role.

学者たるもの、真理の探究に生涯を捧げる覚悟が必要だ。

A scholar, by definition, needs the resolve to devote their life to the pursuit of truth.

Defining a core principle or expectation associated with the role of a scholar.

武士たるもの、主君への忠義を第一と考える。

A samurai (lit: one who is a samurai) considers loyalty to their lord paramount.

Describing a fundamental principle or code of conduct expected of a samurai (often used historically or in fiction).

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • 〜として（は）: Means "as X" or "in the capacity of X". It indicates a role or standpoint but is less prescriptive and doesn't carry the strong emphasis on inherent duties or ideals found in 〜たるもの.
  • 〜である以上 (〜であるいじょう): Means "since/because one is X...". It often leads to a logical conclusion or obligation based on the status, but 〜たるもの focuses more on defining the *essence* or *standard* of the role itself.
  • 〜ともあろう: Focuses on the criticism when someone of a certain status acts *inappropriately* (negative contrast). 〜たるもの focuses on defining the *appropriate* behavior or duties of that status (positive standard).

Cultural and Practical Notes

〜たるもの（は） evokes a sense of duty, honor, and high standards associated with a specific role or position. It is often used when discussing traditional roles, professional ethics, or leadership principles. While grammatically correct, using it in casual conversation would sound overly formal or dramatic.
