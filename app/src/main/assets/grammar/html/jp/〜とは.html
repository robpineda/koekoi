〜とは

Explanation in Original Language

〜はひどい・驚く・すごい。

Meaning and Nuances

"〜とは" (towa) is a particle used to express the speaker's strong reaction – such as surprise, disbelief, admiration, exasperation, disappointment, or condemnation – towards a preceding fact, situation, or statement. It highlights the preceding element as the cause of this strong feeling. Common translations include "To think that ~," "That ~ is ...," "It's incredible/amazing/terrible that ~," or simply marking the surprising element before stating the reaction. It signals that the preceding information is noteworthy and evokes a strong response from the speaker.

Usage in Context

"〜とは" is common in both spoken and written Japanese across various levels of formality, although the reaction that follows might adjust the overall formality. It follows a noun, a verb phrase (usually in plain form), an adjective phrase (plain form), or even a direct quote that represents the surprising or noteworthy information. The sentence often concludes with an explicit statement of the speaker's feeling or evaluation (e.g., `驚いた`, `ひどい`, `信じられない`, `すごい`), or the feeling might be implied by the context. The structure is: `[Fact / Situation / Noun / Clause (plain form)] + とは`.

Examples

彼がそんな嘘をつくとは、信じられない。

That he would tell such a lie... it's unbelievable!

Context: Expressing disbelief and disappointment upon discovering someone lied.

一人で山に登って遭難したとは、無謀なことをしたものだ。

To think he climbed the mountain alone and got lost... what a reckless thing to do.

Context: Expressing criticism or exasperation about someone's reckless behavior and its consequence.

この歳になって新しい言語を学ぶとは、素晴らしいですね。

To learn a new language at this age... that's wonderful, isn't it?

Context: Expressing admiration for someone undertaking a challenging task later in life.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜なんて / 〜なんか (nante / nanka): Also used to express surprise, disbelief, or sometimes contempt, but generally more informal and colloquial than `〜とは`. `なんて` can often replace `とは` in casual speech for similar emotional reactions.

〜というのは (to iu no wa): Primarily used for definitions or explanations ("The thing called X is...", "X means..."). While `〜とは` can also appear in definition questions (e.g., `愛とは何か` - What is love?), the usage described here is specifically for expressing an emotional reaction to a stated fact, which `〜というのは` does not do.

Stating the fact and reaction separately: E.g., `彼が嘘をついた。信じられない。` (He lied. I can't believe it.) Using `〜とは` creates a tighter, more direct link between the fact and the ensuing reaction, emphasizing the fact *as the cause* of the surprise or emotion.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This `〜とは` construction strongly focuses the listener's attention on the preceding statement as the source of the speaker's strong feelings. It's often followed by explicit evaluations like `驚いた` (was surprised), `意外だ` (is unexpected), `ひどい` (is terrible), `残念だ` (is regrettable), `すごい` (is amazing), `うらやましい` (is enviable), `情けない` (is pathetic), etc. Pay attention to the context and the explicit or implicit reaction that follows `〜とは` to understand the specific emotion being conveyed.
