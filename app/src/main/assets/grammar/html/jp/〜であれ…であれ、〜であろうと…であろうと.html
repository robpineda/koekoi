〜であれ…であれ / 〜であろうと…であろうと

Explanation in Original Language

〜でも…でも関係なく、同じ種類のものにはみんな同じことが言える。

Meaning and Nuances

This grammar pattern, appearing as 「Aであれ Bであれ」 or 「Aであろうと Bであろうと」, expresses the idea of "regardless of whether it's A or B," or "be it A or B." It indicates that the statement following it applies equally to both A and B (and often, by implication, to all other similar items or situations). A and B are typically examples drawn from the same category. The structure emphasizes that the specific example (A or B) doesn't change the outcome or the truth of the main statement. It conveys impartiality and universality within the given category. 「〜であろうと」 sounds slightly more emphatic or formal than 「〜であれ」.

Usage in Context

This structure is often used in formal writing, speeches, or discussions when making generalizations or stating principles that apply across different examples within a category. It is typically used with nouns or noun phrases representing the examples (A and B). The structure is: `Noun A + であれ + Noun B + であれ, [Main Clause]` or `Noun A + であろうと + Noun B + であろうと, [Main Clause]`. While grammatically sound, it's less common in casual, everyday conversation.

Examples

社長であれ社員であれ、会社の規則は守らなければならない。

Be it the company president or a regular employee, everyone must follow the company rules.

Stating a rule that applies universally within the company, regardless of position.

晴天であろうと雨天であろうと、祭りは予定通り開催されます。

Whether it's clear weather or rainy weather, the festival will be held as planned.

Announcing that an event will proceed irrespective of the weather conditions.

子供であれ大人であれ、公共の場でのマナーは大切です。

Whether children or adults, manners in public spaces are important.

Emphasizing the universal importance of etiquette for all age groups.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜にしろ〜にしろ / 〜にせよ〜にせよ: Very similar in meaning to 「〜であれ〜であれ」, meaning "whether it's A or B." It might be slightly more common in general usage than 「〜であれ」. Often interchangeable. Example: 「行くにしろ行かないにしろ、連絡してください。」(Whether you go or not, please contact me.)
〜でも〜でも: Can sometimes express a similar idea, but is often simpler and less formal. It can also indicate choice ("either A or B is fine") which 「〜であれ」 does not. Example: 「コーヒーでも紅茶でも、どちらでもいいです。」(Coffee or tea, either is fine.) vs. 「コーヒーであれ紅茶であれ、カフェインが含まれている。」(Be it coffee or tea, it contains caffeine.)
〜を問わず (〜をとわず): Means "regardless of," often used with categories like age, gender, nationality. It usually follows a single category noun rather than listing two examples like 「〜であれ〜であれ」. Example: 「年齢を問わず参加できます。」(You can participate regardless of age.)

Cultural and Practical Notes

This pattern adds a somewhat formal or literary tone to the sentence. It's effective when you want to strongly state that a condition applies universally across different examples within a specific group. The repetition of 「であれ」 or 「であろうと」 emphasizes the "regardless of" aspect. Pay attention to using nouns or noun equivalents for A and B.
