〜であれ…であれ、〜であろうと…であろうと

Explanation in Original Language

〜でも…でも関係なく、同じ種類のものにはみんな同じことが言える。

Meaning and Nuances

This states that regardless of whether it’s 〜 or …, the same applies to all similar things. It’s formal and emphasizes universality within a category.

Usage in Context

Used in formal writing or speech to generalize across options, often philosophically.

Examples

若者であれ老人であれ、健康が大切だ。

Be it young or old, health is important.

Universal truth.

成功であろうと失敗であろうと、努力は無駄ではない。

Whether success or failure, effort is never wasted.

Motivational statement.

日本であれ外国であれ、文化を尊重すべきだ。

Be it Japan or abroad, we should respect culture.

Cultural commentary.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜でも: Simpler “even if,” less formal and categorical.

〜にせよ…にせよ: Similar, but slightly less formal.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Reflects formal Japanese rhetoric of inclusivity. Key for N1 formal reading.
