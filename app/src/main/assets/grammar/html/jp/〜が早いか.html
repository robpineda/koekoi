〜が早いか

Explanation in Original Language

〜するとすぐ続いて次のことが起こる。

Meaning and Nuances

This grammar indicates that as soon as one action (〜) occurs, another follows immediately, often implying a very rapid succession, almost simultaneous occurrence, or a cause-and-effect relationship that happens without any perceptible delay.

Usage in Context

Typically found in written narratives or descriptions, it emphasizes the immediacy of the second action following the first. It often describes sudden changes or reactions.

Examples

ドアを開けるが早いか、強風が部屋に吹き込んできた。

(No sooner had I opened the door than a strong wind blew into the room.)

Context: Describing a sudden, uncontrollable event triggered by opening the door.

彼がマイクを握るが早いか、会場が拍手で沸いた。

(The moment he grabbed the microphone, the audience erupted in applause.)

Context: Highlighting a performer’s immediate impact the instant they took the mic.

目覚ましが鳴るが早いか、彼女はベッドから飛び起きた。

(No sooner had the alarm rung than she leapt out of bed.)

Context: Emphasizing a quick, reflexive reaction to the alarm sound.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜や否や (やいなや): Very similar in meaning and formality, emphasizing immediate succession. Often interchangeable with 〜が早いか.

〜とすぐに: More general and can be used in spoken language. It means "as soon as," but might not carry the same nuance of extreme immediacy or literary feel as 〜が早いか.

〜たとたん(に): Also means "the moment" or "as soon as," but often implies an unexpected or surprising result following the first action.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This structure reflects a Japanese literary tendency to precisely describe sequences of events. While understood, it's less common in everyday conversation compared to simpler structures like 〜たらすぐに or 〜たとたん.
