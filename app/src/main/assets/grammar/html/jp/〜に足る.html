〜に足る

Explanation in Original Language

あるもの・あることが十分〜できる。

Meaning and Nuances

The grammar point "〜に足る" (ni taru) translates to "worthy of ~," "sufficient to ~," or "deserving of ~." It indicates that something (a person, object, action, or situation) meets a certain standard, criteria, or level of quality, making it worth considering, trusting, evaluating, or performing the action mentioned. It often carries a nuance of judgment or evaluation, implying that the subject has inherent value or capability that makes it sufficient for a particular purpose or consideration. It is generally neutral to slightly formal in tone.

Usage in Context

"〜に足る" is more frequently used in written Japanese, formal speeches, articles, or evaluations rather than casual conversation. It typically follows a noun or the stem (連用形 - ren'yōkei) of a verb. The common structure is `Noun + に足る` or `Verb Stem + に足る`. It is used to express that something possesses the necessary qualities for an action or judgment.

Examples

彼の提案は検討するに足る。

His proposal is worthy of consideration.

Context: Discussing various business proposals during a formal meeting.

この美術館はわざわざ訪れるに足る素晴らしいコレクションがある。

This art museum has a wonderful collection that is worth making a special trip to visit.

Context: Recommending a place known for its quality exhibits.

彼は信頼に足る人物だと多くの人が考えている。

Many people consider him to be a person worthy of trust.

Context: Assessing someone's character and reliability.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜だけの価値がある (dake no kachi ga aru): Meaning "has the value of doing ~." While similar, it perhaps focuses more directly on the inherent 'value' rather than 'sufficiency' for a purpose. Often interchangeable with 〜に足る.

〜に値する (ni atai suru): Meaning "deserves ~" or "is worthy of ~." It is a very close synonym and used in similar formal contexts. 〜に足る might sometimes carry a slightly stronger sense of *sufficiency* or meeting a necessary condition, whereas 〜に値する emphasizes inherent worthiness or merit.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This grammar point is often used with verbs related to evaluation, judgment, or trust, such as `信頼する` (to trust), `評価する` (to evaluate), `尊敬する` (to respect), `注目する` (to pay attention to), `検討する` (to consider), `議論する` (to discuss). The negative form, `〜に足らない` (ni taranai), meaning "not sufficient," "not worthy of," or "insignificant," is also commonly used.
