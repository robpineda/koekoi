〜を余儀なくされる、〜を余儀なくさせる

Explanation in Original Language

ある事情によりどうしても〜しなければならなくなる、ある事情が〜という状況に追い込む。

Meaning and Nuances

This pair of expressions describes situations where someone is forced into an action or state by unavoidable external circumstances. They carry a strong nuance of regret or undesirability regarding the forced situation.

  • 〜を余儀なくされる (o yoginaku sareru) [Passive]: "To be forced to (do/undergo)...", "To have no choice but to..." The subject of the sentence is the one being compelled by external factors (e.g., bad weather, illness, economic downturn) to accept an often unwanted situation or action (e.g., cancellation, resignation, change of plans).
  • 〜を余儀なくさせる (o yoginaku saseru) [Causative]: "To force (someone) to...", "To compel..." The subject of the sentence is the external factor or circumstance that forces the situation upon someone else.

Both are very formal expressions, typically used for significant, often negative, changes or decisions brought about by powerful external pressures.

Usage in Context

These expressions are primarily used in formal written language, such as news reports, official announcements, academic papers, and historical accounts. They are suitable for explaining why certain actions (often cancellations, delays, changes in strategy, resignations, evacuations) became unavoidable due to circumstances beyond anyone's control.

The structure usually involves: **Noun (representing the forced action/state) + を余儀なくされる / を余儀なくさせた**

Common nouns include: 中止 (chuushi - cancellation), 変更 (henkou - change), 延期 (enki - postponement), 撤退 (tettai - withdrawal), 辞任 (jinin - resignation), 避難 (hinan - evacuation), 中退 (chuutai - dropping out).

Examples

(〜を余儀なくされる) 大雨の影響で、イベントは中止を余儀なくされた。

Due to the heavy rain, the event was unavoidably cancelled (lit. was forced into cancellation).

Context: A formal announcement or news report explaining the reason for cancelling an event.

(〜を余儀なくされる) 彼は会社の倒産により、転職を余儀なくされた。

He was forced to change jobs due to his company going bankrupt.

Context: Describing a significant life event forced upon someone by economic circumstances.

(〜を余儀なくさせる) 長引く不況は、多くの人々に生活様式の変更を余儀なくさせている。

The prolonged recession is forcing many people to change their lifestyles.

Context: Analyzing the societal impact of economic hardship in a formal report or article.

(〜を余儀なくさせる) 火山の噴火が、周辺住民に避難を余儀なくさせた。

The volcanic eruption forced the surrounding residents to evacuate.

Context: Reporting on the consequences of a natural disaster.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜なければならない / 〜ないといけない: Express general obligation ("must," "have to"). They lack the strong nuance of being *forced by unavoidable, external, often negative circumstances* that is central to 「〜を余儀なくされる/させる」.

〜ないではすまない / 〜ずにはすまない: Indicate unavoidability due to social norms or immediate circumstances ("cannot avoid doing"). Usually refers to specific actions needed to resolve a situation. 「〜を余儀なくされる」 often relates to larger, more impactful life changes or decisions forced by significant events.

〜せざるを得ない (sezaru o enai): Means "cannot avoid doing," "have no choice but to do." It's very similar in meaning to 「〜を余儀なくされる」 and also quite formal. A key difference is structure: 「〜せざるを得ない」 attaches to a verb stem (e.g., 中止せざるを得ない - have no choice but to cancel), while 「〜を余儀なくされる」 typically follows a noun (中止を余儀なくされる - to be forced into cancellation).

Cultural and Practical Notes

These are very formal expressions, almost exclusively used in written contexts or highly formal announcements. They emphasize the powerlessness of the individual or entity in the face of overwhelming external forces (natural disasters, economic conditions, unavoidable circumstances). Understanding the passive (`される` - the one being forced) vs. causative (`させる` - the forcing agent) is key to using them correctly.
