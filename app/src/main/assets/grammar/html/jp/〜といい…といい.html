〜といい…といい

Explanation in Original Language

〜を見ても…を見ても同じような状態だ。

Meaning and Nuances

The pattern 「Aといい Bといい」 is used to pick out two or more representative examples (A and B) to illustrate that a certain quality, state, or evaluation applies to the subject as a whole, based on these examples. It translates roughly to "looking at A, and looking at B..." or "both A and B...". It implies that A and B are typical examples and that the speaker's assessment (often subjective, like praise, criticism, or observation) holds true generally for the thing being described. It frequently carries a nuance of admiration, surprise, dissatisfaction, or emphasis on a particular characteristic evident in both examples.

Usage in Context

「〜といい…といい」 is used when evaluating or commenting on something by highlighting specific aspects. A and B are typically nouns or noun phrases that are parts or characteristics of the subject being discussed. The structure is usually `Noun A + といい + Noun B + といい, [Evaluation/Comment]`. It can be used in both spoken and written Japanese, often adding a touch of emphasis or emotional coloring (positive or negative) to the statement.

Examples

このレストランは、味といいサービスといい、本当に素晴らしい。

This restaurant, both in terms of its taste and its service, is truly wonderful.

Praising a restaurant by highlighting two key positive aspects.

彼のアパートは、広さといい、日当たりの良さといい、申し分ない。

His apartment, in terms of its size and its good sunlight exposure, is perfect (leaves nothing to be desired).

Describing the positive qualities of an apartment using specific examples.

あの選手は、スピードといい、テクニックといい、将来が期待される。

That athlete, considering both their speed and technique, has a promising future.

Evaluating an athlete's potential based on two notable skills.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜も〜も: Simply lists two items that share a characteristic, but without the strong nuance of them being representative examples for an overall evaluation. Example: 「味もサービスも良い。」(The taste and the service are good.) - Less emphatic than 「〜といい〜といい」.
〜だけでなく〜も / 〜はもちろん〜も: Means "not only A but also B." It emphasizes the addition of B to A, rather than using A and B as parallel examples for a general assessment. Example: 「味だけでなくサービスも素晴らしい。」 (Not only the taste but also the service is wonderful.)
〜とか〜とか / 〜やら〜やら: Lists examples, often non-exhaustively or implying variety/disorder, rather than highlighting specific points for evaluation like 「〜といい〜といい」.

Cultural and Practical Notes

「〜といい…といい」 is a useful structure for making evaluations or expressing impressions by citing specific evidence. The choice of A and B should be relevant examples supporting the overall comment. While often used for positive evaluations, it can also be used for negative ones (e.g., 「値段といい、品質といい、がっかりだ。」 - "Both the price and the quality are disappointing."). It adds a slightly more expressive or subjective flavor compared to simply listing characteristics.
