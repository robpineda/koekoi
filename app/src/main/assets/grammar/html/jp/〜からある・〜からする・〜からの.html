〜からある・〜からする・〜からの

Explanation in Original Language

〜かそれ以上の数・量である。

Meaning and Nuances

The patterns 「〜からある (kara aru)」, 「〜からする (kara suru)」, and 「〜からの (kara no)」 all indicate "at least ~," "no less than ~," or "~ or more." They are used after a number + counter to emphasize that the quantity, weight, distance, cost, or number of people is substantial or impressive, meeting or exceeding the stated figure. They often convey a sense of surprise or highlight the largeness of the number.

  • 〜からある: Primarily used for weight, distance, height, size, or physical dimensions.
  • 〜からする: Primarily used for cost or price.
  • 〜からの: Primarily used for a large number of people or items.

Usage in Context

These patterns follow a structure like `[Number + Counter] + からある/からする/からの`. They modify a noun that follows or are used predicatively (e.g., 「重さが50キロからある」 - weighs 50kg or more). The number used is typically significant enough to be considered large or noteworthy in the context. These expressions are common in both spoken and written Japanese when emphasizing quantity.

Examples

会場には、1万人からの観客が集まった。

No less than 10,000 spectators gathered at the venue.

Using 「〜からの」 to emphasize a large number of people.

その宝石は、百万円からする価値があるそうだ。

I hear that jewel is worth at least one million yen.

Using 「〜からする」 to emphasize a high price/value.

港には、５０トンからある巨大なマグロが水揚げされた。

A huge tuna weighing no less than 50 tons was unloaded at the port. (Note: 50 tons is highly unrealistic for a tuna, example emphasizes grammar usage with weight)

Using 「〜からある」 to emphasize a substantial weight (even if the specific example value is exaggerated).

彼は、身長２メートルからある大男だ。

He is a large man, at least 2 meters tall.

Using 「〜からある」 to emphasize significant height.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜以上 (ijou): Means "~ or more." This is a more neutral and common way to express a minimum quantity. 「1万円以上」 (10,000 yen or more). 「〜からある/からする/からの」 add more emphasis on the impressiveness or largeness of the number compared to 「〜以上」.

〜も (mo): Can sometimes imply "as many as" or "as much as" when used with numbers (e.g., 「1万人も集まった」 - As many as 10,000 people gathered). It expresses surprise at the quantity, similar to these patterns, but 「〜からある/からする/からの」 specifically mean "at least that much (and possibly more)" while emphasizing the minimum threshold.

約〜 (yaku ~): Means "approximately ~." This indicates an estimate around the number, whereas 「〜からある/からする/からの」 indicate a minimum value.

Cultural and Practical Notes

These expressions are useful for making quantities sound more impressive or substantial. While 「〜以上」 is neutral, using 「〜からある/からする/からの」 adds a nuance that the speaker considers the number notably large. Remember the specific pairings: weight/distance/size (からある), cost (からする), number of people/items (からの). Mixing them up (e.g., using からする for weight) is incorrect.
