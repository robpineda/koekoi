〜をもって

Explanation in Original Language

〜を手段としてある行為をする。

Meaning and Nuances

The grammar pattern `〜をもって` (o motte) has two primary meanings, both carrying a formal tone:

  1. Means/Method/Instrument: Indicates the tool, method, basis, or manner by which an action is performed. It translates to "with," "by means of," "using," or "based on." It's a formal equivalent of the particle `で`.
  2. Time Limit/End Point: Marks a specific point in time as a boundary, conclusion, or effective date. It translates to "as of," "at," "effective from," or "with (this point in time)."

In both cases, `〜をもって` adds formality compared to more common expressions. The form `〜をもちまして` (o mochimashite) is often used in highly formal situations, particularly for the time limit meaning, to increase politeness.

Usage in Context

`〜をもって` is frequently encountered in formal settings such as official announcements, ceremonies, business documents, written reports, and formal speeches. It's generally not used in casual conversation.

  • When indicating means, it follows a noun representing the means, method, or basis (e.g., 技術をもって - using technology, 誠意をもって - with sincerity).
  • When indicating a time limit, it follows a noun representing a specific date or time (e.g., 本日をもって - as of today, 3月31日をもって - as of March 31st) or a demonstrative like `これ` (kore - this) to signify the conclusion.

Examples

(Means) 全力を挙げて対応する決意をここに表明いたします。誠意をもって問題解決にあたる所存です。

We hereby declare our resolve to respond with all our might. We intend to address the resolution of this issue with sincerity.

A formal statement, perhaps in a press conference or official letter, indicating the manner (sincerity) in which they will act.

(Means) 最新の技術をもってすれば、不可能と考えられていたことも可能になるかもしれない。

By means of the latest technology, things previously thought impossible might become possible.

Discussing potential outcomes using a specific method (latest technology) in a formal or technical context.

(Time Limit) これをもちまして、本日の会議を終了させていただきます。

With this, we shall conclude today's meeting.

A standard, formal phrase used to end a meeting or event, often spoken by the chairperson.

(Time Limit) 当サービスは、2024年12月31日をもって終了いたします。

This service will be terminated as of December 31st, 2024.

A typical formal announcement regarding the end date of a service or operation.

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • Particle `で` (de - Means): The everyday particle for indicating means, tools, or methods (e.g., `バスで` - by bus, `ペンで` - with a pen). `〜をもって` is significantly more formal and often used for more abstract means or in official contexts.

  • Particle `に` (ni - Time): Indicates a specific point in time (e.g., `５時に` - at 5 o'clock). `〜をもって` marks a time as a formal boundary or end point ("as of," "effective from") and is used in announcements or official contexts.

  • 〜によって (ni yotte - Means/Cause): Can indicate means, cause, or agent ("by," "due to," "according to"). While overlapping with the "means" sense of `〜をもって`, `〜をもって` often emphasizes the *instrument* or *method* being actively employed, whereas `〜によって` can have a broader range, including cause or basis.

Cultural and Practical Notes

The time limit meaning of `〜をもって` (especially `〜をもちまして`) is extremely common in formal closings and announcements in Japan (e.g., store closings, event endings). Recognizing this usage is important for understanding official communications. Using `〜をもって` correctly requires understanding its formal register; avoid it in casual settings where `で` or `に` would be appropriate.
