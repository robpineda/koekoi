〜であれ・〜であろうと

Explanation in Original Language

たとえ〜でも、それに関係ない・影響されない

Meaning and Nuances

This grammar pattern means "even if it is Noun/Na-adjective," "regardless of Noun/Na-adjective," or "whoever/whatever Noun may be." It indicates that the statement in the main clause holds true irrespective of the identity, nature, or state described by the preceding noun or na-adjective. It is similar in function to `〜（よ）うと/が` but is used primarily with nouns and na-adjectives, focusing on the state of being (is/am/are).

`〜であれ` is the classical/literary conjunctive form of the formal copula `である`. `〜であろうと` uses the volitional form of `である` (`であろう`) followed by `と`, functioning similarly to `〜（よ）うと`. Both forms carry a formal, literary, or sometimes slightly archaic nuance. They often imply universality, impartiality, or emphasize that the main point applies without exception to the category mentioned.

Usage in Context

This structure is found more often in formal writing, speeches, legal documents, or proverbs rather than everyday conversation. It sets a condition related to identity or state and asserts that the following clause applies regardless. It is often used with words like `何` (what), `誰` (who), `どんな` (what kind of) to mean "whatever," "whoever," "no matter what kind."

Structure: Noun / Na-adjective + であれ / であろうと, [Main Clause]

(For i-adjectives, the related forms `〜かろうと` or `〜くあれ` are used, e.g., `高かろうと` - even if it's expensive).

Examples

理由が何であれ、規則は守らなければならない。

Whatever the reason may be, the rules must be followed.

Stating a strict, non-negotiable rule that applies universally.

たとえ相手が子供であろうと、油断してはいけない。

Even if the opponent is a child, you must not let your guard down.

Giving advice or stating a principle that applies regardless of the opponent's apparent status.

国王であれ、法の下では平等である。

Even if one is a king, they are equal under the law.

Expressing the principle of legal equality, applying even to the highest authority.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜でも: The most basic equivalent for "even if (Noun)." `〜であれ / であろうと` is significantly more formal and literary.

〜（よ）うと/が: Primarily used with verbs to mean "even if (action)." `〜であれ / であろうと` is used with nouns/na-adjectives for states of being.

〜を問わず (〜をとわず): Means "regardless of (Noun)," often used for criteria like age, gender, experience (e.g., `年齢を問わず` - regardless of age). While similar, `〜であれ / であろうと` often sets a more emphatic or hypothetical condition rather than just listing neutral criteria.

〜にしろ・〜にせよ: Can also mean "even if" or "whether," often used with nouns. They are formal but perhaps slightly less literary or archaic-sounding than `〜であれ`.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Understanding that `であれ` and `であろう` derive from the formal copula `である` helps grasp their meaning and formal register. `〜であれ` especially evokes a classical Japanese feel. While not common in casual talk, recognizing these forms is important for understanding formal texts, speeches, and certain idiomatic expressions (like `何であれ` - whatever it may be).
