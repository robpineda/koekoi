〜ながらに（して）

Explanation in Original Language

〜のまま変わらない状態だ。

Meaning and Nuances

This expression means "while in the state of ~," "remaining in the same state of ~," or "from birth/innately." It emphasizes that a certain state or condition has existed continuously from a particular point (often birth or a previous state) without change. It often attaches to nouns or verb stems indicating inherent qualities, states, or conditions.

It can imply something innate, natural, or unchanged over time. The optional して adds a slight emphasis or formality but doesn't significantly alter the meaning. When used with words like 生まれ (umare - birth), 昔 (mukashi - old times), or 涙 (namida - tears), it highlights the persistence of that initial state.

Usage in Context

〜ながらに（して） is typically attached to nouns or the masu-stem of certain verbs. It's often used in slightly formal or literary contexts to describe inherent characteristics or situations that persist unchanged.

Common combinations include:

  • 生まれながらに (umare nagara ni): Innately, since birth
  • 昔ながらに (mukashi nagara ni): In the traditional way, as in the old days
  • 涙ながらに (namida nagara ni): While crying, tearfully
  • 居ながらに (inagara ni): While staying in one place, without moving

Pattern: Noun / Verb Stem + ながらに（して）

Examples

彼は生まれながらにして、優れた音楽の才能を持っていた。

He possessed an outstanding talent for music innately, right from birth.

Emphasizes that his musical talent was natural and present since he was born.

この店は、昔ながらの製法でパンを作り続けている。

This shop continues to make bread using the traditional methods from the old days.

Highlights the unchanged, traditional way the bread is made, preserving the original method.

彼女は涙ながらに、別れの言葉を告げた。

Tearfully (while crying), she said her words of farewell.

Describes the action of speaking *while* in the state of crying.

インターネットのおかげで、居ながらにして世界中の情報を得ることができる。

Thanks to the internet, one can obtain information from all over the world while staying right where they are (without having to go anywhere).

Describes accessing information without changing location, remaining in one place.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜まま (mama): Means "in the state of," "unchanged." It's very similar in meaning but is generally more common and versatile than 〜ながらに. 〜まま can be used more broadly (e.g., 靴を履いたまま - with shoes still on, ドアを開けたまま - with the door left open). 〜ながらに often implies a more inherent or continuous state from a certain point, especially with words like 生まれ or 昔.

Verb-stem + ながら (nagara): This common grammar point means "while doing ~ (simultaneously doing something else)" (e.g., 音楽を聞きながら勉強する - study while listening to music). This is different from 〜ながらに, which indicates a persistent state, not simultaneous actions.

Cultural and Practical Notes

〜ながらに is less frequent in everyday conversation compared to 〜まま or V-stem + ながら. It carries a slightly more formal or literary tone. The most common and important forms to remember are 生まれながらに (innately), 昔ながらに (traditionally), and 涙ながらに (tearfully). Note that ながらに attaches to nouns or verb stems, whereas the common V-stem + ながら attaches only to verb stems.
