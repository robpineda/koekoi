〜にかたくない

Explanation in Original Language

その状況を考えると、実際に見なくても、〜することは難しくない・〜できる。

Meaning and Nuances

The grammar point "〜にかたくない" (ni katakunai) expresses that something is "not difficult to do" or "easy to do," specifically in the sense of imagining, guessing, understanding, or sympathizing based on the given circumstances. It implies that even without direct experience or observation, one can readily comprehend or picture the situation or feeling described.

It often conveys empathy or suggests that a certain conclusion or feeling is a natural consequence of the situation. It is generally used in more formal or written Japanese and carries a slightly literary nuance. It's frequently attached to verbs like 想像する (souzou suru - to imagine), 推察する (suisatsu suru - to guess/infer), 理解する (rikai suru - to understand), or verbs related to emotions.

Usage in Context

This grammar point is primarily found in written language, such as essays, literature, or formal commentaries, rather than casual conversation. It's used when the speaker/writer wants to emphasize the ease with which one can infer or feel something based on the context provided.

The structure is typically:

  • Verb (dictionary form) + にかたくない
  • Noun + (であること) + にかたくない (less common)
It often appears when discussing someone else's feelings (especially hardship or joy) or when drawing a logical conclusion about a situation's difficulty or nature.

Examples

長年の努力が実を結んだ彼の喜びようは、想像にかたくない。

It is not hard to imagine how joyous he was, seeing his many years of effort bear fruit.

Describing the understandable happiness of someone who finally achieved a long-term goal.

愛する家族を失った彼女の悲しみは、察するにかたくない。

It is not difficult to sympathize with the sorrow of her having lost her beloved family.

Expressing empathy for someone experiencing profound grief.

これだけの証拠があれば、彼が犯人であると推察するにかたくない。

Given this much evidence, it is not hard to infer that he is the culprit.

Commenting on the logical conclusion drawn from presented evidence in a formal or analytical setting.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜ことは容易に想像できる (koto wa youi ni souzou dekiru): Means "it's easy to imagine." This is a more straightforward and less literary way to express ease of imagination compared to the slightly more formal and nuanced 〜にかたくない.

〜（こと）は明らかだ ((koto) wa akiraka da): Means "it is clear/obvious." This focuses on the certainty or self-evidence of something, rather than the ease of imagining or understanding it from context.

〜だろう (darou) / 〜でしょう (deshou): Simple speculation ("probably," "I think"). These lack the specific nuance of 〜にかたくない, which emphasizes that the understanding comes easily *given the circumstances*.

Cultural and Practical Notes

The phrase 〜にかたくない often appears when discussing situations that evoke strong emotions, particularly sympathy or understanding of hardship. While it literally means "not difficult," it implies a deeper level of ready comprehension or empathy based on shared human experience or logical deduction from the context.

Learners should be mindful of its formality and avoid using it in casual conversation where simpler expressions like 〜と思う (to omou - I think) or 〜だろう (darou - probably) would be more appropriate.
