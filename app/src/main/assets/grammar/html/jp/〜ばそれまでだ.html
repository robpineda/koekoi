〜ばそれまでだ

Explanation in Original Language

もし〜ということになったら、すべて台無しになってしまい、それ以上は何もできない。

Meaning and Nuances

The expression `〜ばそれまでだ` (ba sore made da) means "if... then it's all over," "if... then that's the end of it," or "if... then everything is ruined/pointless." It signifies that if a certain condition (usually negative or undesirable, indicated by the `〜ば` clause) comes true, then the current effort, situation, value, or potential will be completely nullified, lost, or rendered meaningless. There's nothing more that can be done after that point.

It conveys a strong sense of finality, futility, or serves as a warning about a critical vulnerability or dependency. It implies that the condition mentioned is a crucial breaking point.

Usage in Context

This pattern is used to highlight a critical condition upon which everything else depends, often emphasizing a risk or potential point of failure. It can express resignation about a potential negative outcome or serve as a warning.

The structure typically involves the conditional 'ba' form of a verb, adjective, or the copula 'da' (as 'nara' or 'de areba').

Common structure: Verb (ば form) + それまでだ / い-Adj (-ければ) + それまでだ / な-Adj/Noun (なら・であれば) + それまでだ

Examples

いくら高性能なコンピューターでも、停電してしまえばそれまでだ。

No matter how high-performance a computer is, if the power goes out, it's useless (that's the end of it).

Discussing the dependency of technology on electricity.

どんなに努力しても、試験に合格できなければそれまでだ。

No matter how hard you study, if you can't pass the exam, it's all for nothing.

Expressing the high stakes of an upcoming exam where only passing matters.

素晴らしいアイデアも、実行する資金がなければそれまでだ。

Even a wonderful idea is pointless if there's no funding to implement it.

Highlighting the practical necessity of funding for realizing ideas.

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • 〜たら終わりだ (tara owari da): Meaning "If... it's the end." Very similar in meaning, often interchangeable. `〜ばそれまでだ` might carry a slightly stronger nuance of the preceding effort or value becoming *meaningless* or *nullified*, rather than just reaching an end point.
  • 〜て（は）おしまいだ (te (wa) oshimai da): Also means "it's the end if..." or "it's all over if...". Similar to the others, but `〜ばそれまでだ` particularly emphasizes the loss of value accumulated or effort expended *up to that point*.
  • 〜たら元も子もない (tara moto mo ko mo nai): Meaning "If... you lose everything (principal and interest)." This idiom specifically emphasizes losing both the initial investment/effort and any potential gains, suggesting total loss. It's similar in conveying disastrous consequences but more specific about losing everything invested.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This expression is quite potent in highlighting a single point of failure or a critical dependency. It's frequently used in warnings, risk assessments, or expressions of pessimistic realism. It underscores the fragility of a situation or plan based on one specific condition.
