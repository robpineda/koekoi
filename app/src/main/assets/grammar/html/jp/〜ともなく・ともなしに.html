〜ともなく・ともなしに

Explanation in Original Language

A：自分で〜しようとはっきり意識しないまま、ある動作を行う。B：はっきり〜と特定できない。

Meaning and Nuances

This expression has two main meanings:

A) Doing something without conscious intention or specific purpose: This usage implies performing an action idly, aimlessly, or without really thinking about it. It often applies to perception verbs like見る (miru - to see), 聞く (kiku - to hear/listen), or general actions like 歩く (aruku - to walk) or 待つ (matsu - to wait). It conveys a sense of casualness, lack of focus, or acting on autopilot.

B) Indefinite or unspecified source/time/place: This usage involves question words (like どこ - doko/where, いつ - itsu/when, だれ - dare/who) and indicates that the specific source, time, or place is unknown or uncertain. It means something like "from somewhere or other," "at some time or other," or "by someone or other."

The form 〜ともなしに is slightly more emphasized or literary than 〜ともなく, but they are largely interchangeable.

Usage in Context

For Meaning A: It typically follows the dictionary form of a verb, often repeated (e.g., 見るともなく見る - miru tomo naku miru - to watch without really watching). It describes actions done passively or without clear intent, often in moments of relaxation, contemplation, or distraction.

For Meaning B: It follows a question word (どこからともなく - doko kara tomo naku - from somewhere unspecified). It's used when the origin or specifics are vague or unimportant.

Both usages tend to appear more in written language or slightly formal/narrative speech than in very casual conversation.

Examples

(Meaning A) テレビを見るともなく見ていたら、突然懐かしい友人が映った。

While idly watching TV without really paying attention, an old friend suddenly appeared on screen.

Describes watching TV passively, without specific intention, leading to an unexpected discovery.

(Meaning A) 電車を待つともなしにホームに立っていると、昔の恋人に偶然会った。

As I stood on the platform, not really consciously waiting for the train (just standing there), I unexpectedly ran into my ex.

Describes being present without a strong focus on the act of waiting, leading to a chance encounter.

(Meaning B) どこからともなく、美しい花の香りが漂ってきた。

From somewhere or other (unspecified), the beautiful scent of flowers came wafting.

Describes smelling something pleasant without knowing the exact source location.

(Meaning B) いつからともなく、その猫はこの家に住み着くようになった。

From some unspecified time onwards, that cat came to live in this house.

Describes a situation where the exact starting point is unknown or vague.

Comparison to Similar Structures

Meaning A (Lack of Intention):

〜とはなしに: Very similar to 〜ともなしに, often used interchangeably for meaning A. Some sources suggest 〜とはなしに might slightly emphasize the lack of a specific *partner* in an action (like 話すとはなしに - talking without a specific listener), but this distinction is subtle.

何気なく (nanigenaku - casually, without thinking): Similar in meaning, conveying unintentional action. 何気なく is perhaps more common in everyday speech and acts as a simple adverb. 〜ともなく often appears with the verb repeated (見る Vるともなく Vる), adding a slightly more literary feel.

Meaning B (Indefinite Source):

どこかから (doko ka kara - from somewhere), いつか (itsu ka - sometime): These are simpler ways to express indefiniteness. 〜ともなく adds a slightly more literary or poetic nuance, emphasizing the vagueness or mysteriousness of the origin/time.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Recognize the two distinct meanings based on whether the preceding word is a verb (Meaning A) or a question word (Meaning B). When used with a verb (Meaning A), repeating the verb (VるともなくVる) is a common pattern. While grammatically correct, using this pattern too frequently in casual conversation might sound a bit stilted or overly descriptive.
