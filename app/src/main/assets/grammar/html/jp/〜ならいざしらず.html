〜ならいざしらず

Explanation in Original Language

〜ならそうかもしれないが、全く違う状況なのだから結果も違う。

Meaning and Nuances

This means “if it were 〜, perhaps, but since it’s not, the outcome differs.” It contrasts a hypothetical with reality, often dismissively.

Usage in Context

Used in speech or writing to reject an assumption or highlight situational differences, often critically.

Examples

若者ならいざしらず、彼はもう年を取っている。

If he were young, maybe, but he’s already old.

Age critique.

専門家ならいざしらず、私には無理だ。

If I were an expert, perhaps, but I can’t do it.

Self-deprecation.

金持ちならいざしらず、普通の人には高すぎる。

If you were rich, maybe, but for ordinary people, it’s too expensive.

Economic reality.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜ならまだしも: Similar contrast, but less dismissive.

〜でなければ: Simpler “if not,” without nuance.

Cultural and Practical Notes

Reflects pragmatic Japanese reasoning. Key for N1 argumentative reading.
