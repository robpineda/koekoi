〜ときたら

Explanation in Original Language

〜は良くない。

Meaning and Nuances

The expression 〜ときたら is used to single out a topic (often a person, group, or situation) and express criticism, complaint, disappointment, or exasperation about it. It literally translates to something like "Speaking of X..." or "When it comes to X...", but carries a strong negative connotation, implying that the speaker finds the topic problematic or unsatisfactory. It's primarily used in informal, spoken Japanese.

It highlights a negative aspect or behavior associated with the noun preceding it. The feeling conveyed is often one of annoyance, frustration, or resigned complaint.

Usage in Context

〜ときたら is typically used in informal conversations when complaining, venting, or expressing dissatisfaction. It follows a noun or noun phrase that identifies the subject of the complaint.

The common sentence structure is:

[Noun/Topic] + ときたら + [Negative Comment / Complaint / Frustrating Situation]

It's rarely used in formal writing or speech due to its complaining and informal tone.

Examples

うちの息子ときたら、毎日ゲームばかりして、全然勉強しないんだ。

Speaking of my son, he just plays games all day, every day, and doesn't study at all.

A parent complaining to a friend or family member about their son's habits, expressing frustration.

最近の若い人たちときたら、礼儀を知らない人が多くて困る。

When it comes to young people these days, it's troubling how many don't know basic manners.

An older person expressing a general complaint or stereotypical view about the younger generation's etiquette.

このアパートの壁ときたら、薄すぎて隣の音が全部聞こえるよ。

As for the walls in this apartment, they're so thin you can hear everything from next door.

A tenant complaining about the poor soundproofing in their apartment building.

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • 〜は: The basic topic marker. It's neutral and simply introduces a topic. 〜ときたら is specifically used to introduce a topic *for the purpose of complaining* or expressing negativity about it.
  • 〜といえば: Means "Speaking of X..." and is used to introduce a topic that came to mind, often based on the preceding conversation. It's generally neutral and doesn't inherently carry the negative/complaining nuance of 〜ときたら.
  • 〜なんて / 〜なんか: Can express negative feelings, surprise, or contempt towards the preceding noun, but often focuses on the noun itself being bad or unbelievable. 〜ときたら focuses more on bringing up the topic to launch into a complaint about associated behavior or situations.

Cultural and Practical Notes

〜ときたら strongly signals a complaining or critical stance. Using it directly about someone present can be considered rude, unless done playfully among very close friends or family. It's a very colloquial expression that adds emotional weight (specifically negative) to the statement, effectively saying, "Let me tell you how bad/annoying X is..."
