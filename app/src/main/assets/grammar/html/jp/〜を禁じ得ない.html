〜を禁じ得ない

Explanation in Original Language

ある事態にあって、〜という感情がわいてくるのを抑えられない。

Meaning and Nuances

「〜を禁じ得ない」(o kinji enai) means "cannot help but feel," "cannot suppress (a feeling)," or "unable to restrain..." It describes a situation where a particular emotion arises spontaneously and involuntarily in response to something observed or experienced, and the speaker finds it impossible to hold it back. This expression is quite formal and often found in written language or formal speeches. It typically applies to specific, often palpable emotional reactions like tears (涙), laughter (笑い), anger (怒り), sympathy (同情), doubt (疑念), or surprise (驚き).

Usage in Context

This grammar point is characteristic of formal writing (essays, reports, literature) and very formal speech. It is used to describe an uncontrollable, spontaneous emotional reaction triggered by a specific event, sight, or piece of information. It emphasizes the involuntary nature of the feeling welling up.

The structure is: **Noun (usually an emotion) + を禁じ得ない**

Common nouns used include: 涙 (namida - tears), 笑い (warai - laughter), 失笑 (shisshou - chuckle/smirk), 怒り (ikari - anger), 憤り (ikidoori - indignation), 同情 (doujou - sympathy), 疑念 (ginen - doubt), 驚き (odoroki - surprise), 感嘆 (kantan - admiration).

Examples

彼の熱意あふれるスピーチに、感嘆の念を禁じ得なかった。

I couldn't help but feel admiration for his passionate speech.

Context: Describing one's involuntary feeling of admiration after listening to a very moving speech.

その子供たちの無邪気な笑顔を見ていると、こちらも微笑みを禁じ得ない。

Watching the innocent smiles of those children, I can't help but smile myself.

Context: Explaining a natural, happy reaction to seeing children playing.

あまりにも理不尽な決定に対し、強い憤りを禁じ得ない。

I cannot suppress my strong indignation towards such an unreasonable decision.

Context: Formally expressing strong, uncontrollable anger about an unfair ruling or policy.

彼の話の矛盾点に、疑念を禁じ得なかった。

I couldn't help but feel doubt about the contradictions in his story.

Context: Describing the involuntary skepticism that arose while listening to someone speak.

Comparison to Similar Structures

〜に堪えない (ni taenai): Also expresses overwhelming, unbearable emotion. However, 〜に堪えない focuses more on the extreme *intensity* and *unbearability* of the feeling itself. 「〜を禁じ得ない」 focuses more on the *spontaneous emergence* of the feeling and the inability to *prevent* it from showing.

〜てやまない (te yamanai): Expresses a deep, *persistent* feeling or wish held over time (e.g., hope, respect, prayer). 「〜を禁じ得ない」 describes a more *immediate, involuntary reaction* to a specific, present stimulus.

思わず〜してしまった (omowazu ~shite shimatta): Means "unintentionally ended up doing ~." It describes an involuntary action or utterance, often less formal than 「〜を禁じ得ない」. While both indicate lack of control, 「〜を禁じ得ない」 specifically targets the inability to suppress an *internal feeling* from arising or showing.

Cultural and Practical Notes

「〜を禁じ得ない」 is a very formal and somewhat literary expression. Using it in casual conversation would sound overly stiff or dramatic. It's most effective in writing or formal speeches when describing a powerful, spontaneous emotional response that feels beyond one's conscious control. The noun preceding it clearly identifies the specific emotion that could not be suppressed.
