〜ゆえ（に）

Explanation in Original Language

〜から（理由）

Meaning and Nuances

`〜ゆえ（に）` (or `〜故（に）`) is a relatively formal or literary way to express reason or cause, similar to "because of," "due to," or "therefore." It indicates a direct causal link between the part preceding `ゆえ（に）` and the result or situation that follows. Compared to more common causal markers like `から` or `ので`, `ゆえ（に）` often carries a more formal, written, slightly archaic, or sometimes philosophical or profound nuance. `〜ゆえに` (with に) often explicitly links to the resulting clause, while `〜ゆえの` modifies a following noun.

Usage in Context

This pattern is primarily found in written language (literature, formal essays, official documents, proverbs) and formal speeches. It is less common in everyday conversation. It connects a reason (noun, noun phrase + `の`, or adjective/verb clause) to a result.

  • Noun + （の） + ゆえ（に）
  • な-Adjective + な + ゆえ（に）
  • い-Adjective (plain form) + ゆえ（に）
  • Verb (plain form) + ゆえ（に）

When used as `〜ゆえの + Noun`, it means "Noun resulting from/caused by ~".

Examples

若さゆえの過ちというものもある。

There are such things as mistakes made due to youth.

A general observation, often used reflectively or philosophically, about the kinds of errors attributable to inexperience.

悪天候ゆえに、本日の試合は中止となりました。

Due to the bad weather, today's match has been cancelled.

A formal announcement explaining the reason for the cancellation of an event.

彼は外国人であるがゆえに、日本の習慣に慣れていない面がある。

Because he is a foreigner, there are aspects of Japanese customs he is not accustomed to.

Explaining someone's unfamiliarity with local customs in a slightly formal or objective manner.

貧しさゆえに、十分な教育を受けられなかった。

Due to poverty, I (or someone) could not receive sufficient education.

Stating a serious reason for a significant life outcome, often in a narrative or formal context.

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • 〜から / 〜ので: The most common ways to express reason/cause in everyday Japanese. `〜ゆえに` is significantly more formal and less common in conversation.
  • 〜ため（に）: Also used for reason/cause, often in formal or written contexts, but generally sounds more neutral and less literary or archaic than `〜ゆえに`.
  • 〜せい（で）: Used specifically for negative reasons/causes ("due to the fault of..."). `〜ゆえに` is neutral and can be used for positive, negative, or neutral reasons.
  • 〜おかげで: Used specifically for positive reasons/causes ("thanks to...").

Cultural and Practical Notes

`〜ゆえ（に）` adds a touch of formality, literary flair, or sometimes gravitas to a statement of reason. While grammatically straightforward, its use is stylistically marked. Using it in casual conversation might sound overly formal, pretentious, or old-fashioned. It's most appropriate in writing, formal speeches, or when quoting proverbs or established sayings (e.g., 人は考える葦である故に偉大である - Man is great because he is a thinking reed).
