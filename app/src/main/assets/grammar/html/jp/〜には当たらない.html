〜には当たらない

Explanation in Original Language

それほど大したことではないから、〜するのは適当ではない。

Meaning and Nuances

The grammar point `〜には当たらない` (ni wa ataranai) translates to "it's not worth...", "there's no reason to...", "it's not appropriate to...", or "it doesn't warrant...". It indicates that a particular reaction, judgment, or action (such as surprise, praise, criticism, worry, thanks) is excessive, uncalled for, or inappropriate given the circumstances. The speaker believes the situation or action being responded to is not significant, unusual, or blameworthy enough to justify that specific response.

It often functions to downplay the significance of an event or action, or to moderate an emotional or judgmental response.

Usage in Context

This pattern is used to suggest that a reaction is disproportionate to the cause. It is frequently employed in situations requiring politeness, modesty, or objective assessment.

Common structures:

  • Verb (Dictionary Form) + には当たらない
  • Noun + には当たらない

It is often used with verbs/nouns related to judgment, emotion, or evaluation, such as: 驚く (odoroku - be surprised), 感心する (kanshin suru - admire), 称賛する (shousan suru - praise), 非難する (hinan suru - criticize), 心配する (shinpai suru - worry), 感謝する (kansha suru - thank), 怒る (okoru - get angry).

Examples

彼が正直に謝罪したのだから、それ以上非難するには当たらないでしょう。

Since he apologized honestly, it's probably not appropriate to criticize him further.

Suggesting that further criticism is unwarranted after someone has already apologized sincerely.

今回はたまたま運が良かっただけです。称賛には当たりません。

This time I was just lucky, that's all. It's not worthy of praise.

Modestly downplaying one's own success when receiving compliments.

その程度のことで驚くには当たりませんよ。もっとすごいことが起こるかもしれません。

It's not worth being surprised over something of that level. Something even more amazing might happen.

Calming someone down who is overreacting to a relatively minor event.

道に迷っただけなので、心配するには及びません。（心配するには当たらない is also possible but 及びません is common here)

I just got lost, so there's no need to worry.

Reassuring someone who is worried about them being late.

Comparison to Similar Structures

  • 〜ほどではない (hodo de wa nai): Means "not to the extent of..." or "not that...". This focuses more on the *degree* being less than expected or implied (e.g., "It's not *that* difficult"). `〜には当たらない` focuses specifically on the *appropriateness* or *justification* of a particular *reaction* or *judgment*.
  • 〜必要はない (hitsuyou wa nai): Means "there is no need to...". This expresses a general lack of necessity. `〜には当たらない` is more specific, arguing against a certain *type* of response (like praise, blame, surprise) as being unsuitable for the situation.
  • 〜（する）には及ばない (ni wa oyobanai): Often used similarly to mean "there's no need to...", especially with actions like worrying (心配するには及ばない) or thanking (感謝するには及びません). It can be very close in meaning to `〜には当たらない` in these contexts, often implying the situation isn't serious enough to warrant the action.

Cultural and Practical Notes

This expression is valuable in social interactions in Japan, particularly for showing modesty (deflecting praise) or for handling potentially sensitive situations diplomatically (mitigating criticism or worry). It helps maintain social harmony by framing reactions as potentially excessive.

It can also be used for objective analysis, stating that a phenomenon doesn't meet the criteria for a certain label or reaction.
